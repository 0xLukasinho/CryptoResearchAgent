# Bitcoin ETF Inflows: My Research Note

## Personal observation, March 2026

I've been watching the Bitcoin ETF flows on a weekly basis since launch and a few patterns are starting to crystallize that aren't widely discussed:

**1. The "second wave" thesis is wrong.** When BlackRock's IBIT crossed $50B AUM, market commentators expected a fresh retail wave. It didn't materialize. Instead, what we're seeing is *internal rotation* — institutional allocators rebalancing from Grayscale's GBTC into IBIT and FBTC for fee compression. The flow numbers look bullish but the *net new money* into Bitcoin is much smaller than headline AUM growth suggests.

**2. ETF flows are now a leading indicator for spot price, but only on the downside.** When ETF inflows turn negative (3+ consecutive trading days of net outflows), spot price drops within ~5 trading days about 78% of the time in my dataset (n=14 occurrences since Jan 2024). The reverse — strong inflows preceding spot rallies — only correlates about 41% of the time.

**3. The seasonal pattern shifted.** Pre-ETF, Bitcoin's seasonal weakness was concentrated in September. With ETF flows now anchored to traditional finance calendars, weakness has migrated to mid-November (year-end tax-loss harvesting by institutional holders) and late-March (fiscal year reset for some funds).

## Implications for the recovery thesis

If "ETF inflows signal market recovery" is the working thesis, the data suggests we should be more careful about *what kind* of inflow:

- **Net new institutional capital** (not GBTC→IBIT rotation) → genuine recovery signal
- **Sustained 4+ week inflow streaks** → stronger predictor than single-week numbers
- **Coincident inflows + falling exchange balances** → highest-confidence recovery signal

Single-week ETF inflow numbers in isolation are too noisy to be useful. The "recovery signal" framing needs a multi-factor confirmation.

## Open questions

- Does the 78% downside-correlation hold during macro risk-off events, or only during crypto-specific drawdowns?
- How does the ETF flow signal interact with on-chain metrics like LTH-SOPR or MVRV-Z?
- Is there a leading indicator for ETF outflows themselves (e.g., specific basis-trade unwinds)?

These are the threads I'd most want a research article to pull on.
